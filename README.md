# Customer Retention vs. Acquisition Decision Memo

A MECE and hypothesis-driven analysis of credit card customer churn, built to size the
retention-vs-acquisition budget tradeoff facing retail credit card businesses.

**Approach:** Broke the churn problem into 3 MECE drivers (engagement decline, product
utilization, service friction), tested a falsifiable hypothesis for each against a
10,127-customer dataset, then segmented customers by value and churn risk to size the
at-risk portion of the portfolio.

**Key finding:** 12.7% of total portfolio credit value sits in the High Value / High Risk
segment - customers the business extended the most credit to, who have already churned.
